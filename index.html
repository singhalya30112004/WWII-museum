Our Exhibitions

Explore the world's largest collection of WWII books, films, music, documents, art, and more!

BOOKS

The outbreak of war in 1939, as in 1914, brought to an end an era of great intellectual and creative exuberance. The rationing of paper affected the production of magazines and books. Discover more about this unique period in world literature!

FILMS AND MUSIC

During World War II, the entertainment industry changed to help the war effort. It became more closely controlled by national governments, who sought to keep spirits high and to depict the war in a positive light. They also used entertainment media to keep citizens informed. Discover more about this unique period in entertainment media!

IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS

Official documents from World War II offer a glimpse into the political landscape, societal structures, and economic turmoil of the time. Ration cards, memos, declarations of war, treaties, plans, wills and more! Discover it all here at the archives of the World War II Museum!

PROMINENT LEADERS

Learn more about the leaders who shaped this era and get a glance at some prominent faces. The World War II Museum's collection stands unparalleled!

ART AND SCULPTURES

During World War II, many artists found themselves in the most difficult conditions, and their works are a testimony to a powerful "urge to create." Sculptures too came up often as a tribute to fallen soldiers from the war and an ode to the nation.
